The Arthashastra: Ancient Indian Statecraft

Published: September 23, 2025

Introduction

The Arthashastra is an ancient Indian treatise on politics, economics, and statecraft, written by Kautilya (Chanakya). It is one of the earliest works of political philosophy in the world.

The Author: Kautilya

Kautilya, also known as Chanakya or Vishnugupta, was the advisor and mentor of Chandragupta Maurya. His strategies were pivotal in founding the Mauryan Empire.

Concept of Trayi

In the Arthashastra, Kautilya describes the concept of Trayi, which refers to the threefold Vedic knowledge. This text draws wisdom from the Rigveda, Samaveda, and Yajurveda.

Conclusion

The Arthashastra remains a timeless guide on leadership, ethics, and governance. Its lessons are still relevant in today’s political and economic world.
